## 1. The Sendai-Tobu Expressway

During the Great East Japan Earthquake, the Sendai-Tobu Expressway—running north–south through the eastern part of Sendai—played a critical role in saving local residents from the massive tsunami that struck the Pacific coast. Constructed as an embankment approximately 7–10 meters high, the road functioned as a barrier, reducing the inflow of tsunami waters and debris into inland areas.

Between 1994 and 2001, the 24.8 km section of the Sendai-Tobu Expressway between Watari IC and Sendai-Kokita IC was fully opened to traffic. At that time, the Sanriku Expressway had already been opened up to Ishinomaki-Kanan IC, and the Sendai-Tobu Expressway and the Sanriku Expressway were operated in an integrated manner. Subsequently, prior to the disaster, the Sanriku Expressway was extended to Tome-Towa IC, while the Joban Expressway was extended to Yamamoto IC.

## 2. The Great East Japan Earthquake and the Sendai-Tobu Expressway

On March 11, 2011, the tsunami generated by the Great East Japan Earthquake struck the Pacific coast of eastern Japan. The coastal areas of Sendai also suffered catastrophic damage.

Under these circumstances, the embankment structure of the Sendai-Tobu Expressway helped mitigate tsunami damage in the densely populated residential areas located on its western side. In addition, it functioned as a temporary evacuation site. In 2010, a petition signed by approximately 15,000 residents was submitted requesting that the expressway be designated as a temporary evacuation site, and discussions were ongoing through symposiums. Although it had not yet been officially designated, approximately 230 people evacuated onto the expressway during the tsunami.

## 3. Multi-layered Defense System after the Great East Japan Earthquake

These lessons were incorporated into tsunami countermeasures in the Sendai Reconstruction Plan. As a result, roads located on the seaward side (east side) of the Sendai-Tobu Expressway, such as the Shiogama-Watari Line, were elevated.

The route and height of these elevated roads, with a total length of 10.2 km, were determined based on tsunami inundation simulations conducted by Tohoku University. The design ensures that, even under a tsunami of the same scale as the Great East Japan Earthquake, inundation depths on the inland side remain below 2 meters.

To facilitate smooth evacuation, intersections with evacuation routes were designed by widening the evacuation roads, allowing evacuation vehicles to pass through intersections smoothly, including the use of zebra zones. In addition, structural measures were introduced at intersections and waterways to control openings and reduce tsunami inflow.

The completed elevated roads are positioned as a key component of a “multi-layered defense system,” following coastal levees and coastal protection forests. Together with evacuation facilities, they play an essential role in ensuring safety and security in the eastern coastal areas of Sendai.
